# Microsoft Discovery Cognition

Cognition provides an ongoing reasoning process on the work you are performing and acts cooperatively with you to assign tasks, review work, open new questions, and otherwise continue moving forward in the direction you set for this effort. Unlike traditional AI assistants that respond to individual prompts, cognition operates continuously in the background, maintaining awareness of your overall objectives while autonomously managing the tactical execution of work.

## Overview

Think of cognition as a capable colleague working alongside you on long-duration projects. While you focus on strategic direction, domain expertise, and quality judgment, cognition handles the ongoing execution: selecting appropriate tools, sequencing work, managing dependencies, retrying failed approaches, and synthesizing results across multiple activities.

Cognition is designed to operate over hours and days rather than seconds and minutes, making it ideal for complex, multi-faceted problems that require sustained effort and exploration.

## Enabling Cognition

Cognition is enabled by turning on "Discovery Mode." Once enabled, the cognition system:

1. **Monitors your task list**: Reviews all tasks, their descriptions, validation requirements, and dependencies
2. **Starts autonomous work**: Begins executing tasks that are ready to start based on their dependencies
3. **Maintains continuous awareness**: Keeps track of overall objectives while working on specific sub-tasks
4. **Adapts to feedback**: Incorporates your comments, edits, and new tasks into its ongoing reasoning

Cognition works off of the task list you create either by manually adding tasks or by asking the universal chat for a high-level objective while in Discovery Mode.

## What Cognition Does

### Autonomous Task Management

When cognition is active, it continuously:

- **Decomposes high-level objectives** into actionable sub-tasks
- **Sequences work intelligently** based on task dependencies and priorities
- **Parallelizes independent tasks** to make efficient progress
- **Identifies blockers** and either resolves them or flags them for your attention
- **Manages task lifecycle** from planning through execution to completion

### Intelligent Resource Selection

Cognition autonomously selects and orchestrates:

- **Agents**: Chooses appropriate agents based on task requirements and agent capabilities
- **Models**: Selects models optimized for specific reasoning or generation tasks
- **Tools**: Identifies and invokes tools needed for data access, computation, or analysis
- **Knowledge sources**: Queries bookshelves and data assets relevant to the current work

### Adaptive Execution

The cognition system demonstrates scientific and engineering rigor by:

- **Trying multiple approaches**: If one method fails, cognition explores alternatives
- **Learning from results**: Incorporates findings from completed tasks into future decisions
- **Maintaining context**: Remembers details from earlier work within the investigation that inform later tasks
- **Escalating when stuck**: Recognizes when it needs additional guidance or information

### Collaborative Awareness

Cognition recognizes that you're an active participant:

- **Sees your work**: If you complete a task manually or provide intermediate results, cognition incorporates that
- **Responds to feedback**: Comments you add to tasks guide future execution
- **Adapts to changes**: Task modifications, priority shifts, and new tasks are immediately reflected in cognition's planning
- **Builds on your contributions**: Work you do becomes part of the shared context for ongoing efforts

## What to Expect

### Activity Patterns

When cognition is on you will see activity begin happening autonomously:

- **New sub-tasks appearing**: Cognition breaks down your high-level objectives
- **Tasks moving through statuses**: From "Not Started" to "In Progress" to "Completed"
- **Execution history growing**: Records of attempts, approaches, and results
- **Data assets being created**: Outputs from completed tasks
- **Agent and tool invocations**: Background work happening on your behalf

This is cognition working on your behalf and taking action to further your goals.

### Timeframe and Rhythm

Cognition operates on a different timescale than interactive chat:

- **Hours and days, not seconds**: It depends on the tasks at hand, complex work takes time to explore properly
- **Check in periodically**: Review progress every few hours rather than watching constantly
- **Asynchronous collaboration**: You don't need to stay engaged - cognition continues while you focus elsewhere
- **Natural breakpoints**: Review completed tasks, provide feedback, then let cognition continue

### Progress Monitoring

You can track what cognition is doing through:

- **Task status updates**: See which tasks are in progress, blocked, or completed
- **Execution history**: Detailed records of what was tried and what was found
- **Data assets**: Outputs and intermediate results from completed work
- **Comments and notes**: System-generated observations about progress or issues

## How to Interact with Cognition

### Strategic Direction

Your primary role is providing guidance, not instructions:

- **Set objectives**: Define what you want to achieve and why it matters
- **Establish priorities**: Indicate which tasks or areas are most important
- **Provide context**: Share domain knowledge and constraints that guide decisions
- **Define success**: Specify validation requirements that clarify what "done" means

### Feedback and Refinement

Feel free to engage by:

- **Reviewing completed work**: Check results against your expectations
- **Providing feedback**: Add comments explaining what's good or what needs adjustment
- **Modifying sub-tasks**: Edit descriptions, validation requirements, or priorities
- **Adding new tasks**: Introduce new directions or areas to explore
- **Canceling irrelevant tasks**: Mark tasks as blocked or cancelled if they're no longer needed

### Collaborative Work

If you are working towards a part of the broader objective, cognition will see your work too and build on top of it. You can:

- **Complete tasks manually**: Mark tasks as done with your own results
- **Provide partial results**: Add data assets or execution notes that inform future work
- **Work in parallel**: Focus on high-priority tasks while cognition handles others
- **Course-correct in real-time**: Adjust direction as new information emerges

## Best Practices

### Trust and Autonomy

- **Let cognition explore**: Don't micromanage every decision - allow autonomous exploration of approaches
- **Provide outcomes, not procedures**: Specify what you want achieved, not step-by-step instructions
- **Review periodically, not constantly**: Check in at natural breakpoints rather than hovering

### Effective Communication

- **Use tasks as the primary interface**: Add tasks for new objectives rather than trying to guide through chat
- **Write clear validation requirements**: Concrete success criteria help cognition make good decisions
- **Add context through comments**: Explain why something matters or what you're looking for
- **Link related tasks**: Dependencies and relationships provide important context

### Iteration and Refinement

- **Start broad, refine based on results**: Let cognition do initial exploration, then provide guidance
- **Treat it as a multi-day process**: Complex work benefits from time to explore and iterate
- **Provide feedback on what matters**: Focus on strategic direction rather than tactical choices
